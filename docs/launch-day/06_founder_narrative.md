I built Trinity Local because I had three AI subscriptions open in three terminals and was copy-pasting between them like an animal. That's the hook. I came up through engineering at IIT Kharagpur, design thinking at Harvard GSD, and shipped the propensity models that ran behind Mailchimp's early growth — the through-line is that I've spent twenty years building the layer that sits between the customer's intent and the system that serves it. Trinity is that layer for the polyharness era. The wedge is structural, not technical: Anthropic isn't allowed to recommend ChatGPT, OpenAI isn't allowed to recommend Claude, and the moment you have more than one subscription, the cross-provider memory layer cannot come from a lab. It has to come from someone outside. So I shipped it. The folder is yours. The taste is yours.
