Title: Design the Affordance
Source: thinking notes — themes extracted, no private content surfaced

Core claims (the user's own ideas, scrubbed of any private chat context):

- "It's a design problem, not a prediction problem."
- "Behavior is not goal-driven; it's a structural affordance."
- "The motivation industry — incentives, KPIs, OKRs, rewards, gamification —
  is 70 years of symptom management."
- "Behavior emerges from structure."
- Six moves replace the entire incentive layer:
  1. Name the roles
  2. Enforce the boundaries
  3. Put signal in its channel
  4. Cheap path = right path
  5. Narrow waist between layers
  6. Self-correction built in
- "The lever is always one layer deeper than people are looking."
- Arc of the six moves: foundation (roles) → substrate (boundaries) →
  content (signal) → dynamics (cheap path) → composability (waist) →
  resilience (self-correction). Static → dynamic → systemic.
